InterGDance: Prior-guided Music-driven Interactable Group Choreography
in a Number-Free Paradigm


Anonymous submission   

Abstract

Music-driven group dance choreography has garnered attention in both cultural entertainment and academia. Recent works mainly confront two crucial shortcomings, on the one hand, they lack interaction annotations, therefore can not learn a more diverse and immersive user experience; on the other hand, most existing methods can not achieve \textbf{number-free generation}, which means they can not generate group dance with a various number of dancers. To address these issues, we present InterGDance, a framework that integrates interaction priors for motion control and enables number-free generation. Our main motivation is employing human priors to efficiently guide the interaction and expansion of dancers. Specifically, to overcome the lack of interaction annotations, we introduce a rule-based method for classifying group dance patterns and labeling interactions into distinct categories. To achieve number-free generation, we propose Expansion Sampling to maintain harmonious dancer movements when extending the number of participants. Extensive experiments demonstrate that our method achieves state-of-the-art results.
