InterGDance: Music-Driven Interaction-Controllable Group Choreography


Anonymous submission   

Abstract

Music-driven group dance choreography has gained significant attention in both cultural entertainment and academia. Unlike solo dance, the interactions among participants in larger ensembles foster a more immersive experience. However, existing methods either fail to scale effectively in large groups or struggle to preserve interaction patterns. To address this, we introduce InterGDance, a highly scalable framework that enables control over interactions. To ensure scalability, we propose a novel approach that scales to an arbitrary number of dancers by progressively adding new dancers based on the existing ensemble. To facilitate interaction design, we introduce an interaction-controllable generation approach. Additionally, we present a rule-based method for automatically identifying interaction patterns, which can both provide interaction priors and enable the automated evaluation of whether the generated results conform to specific interaction patterns. Extensive experiments demonstrate that our method achieves state-of-the-art results. Code will be made available.
